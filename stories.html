1. The Gada System: The Heartbeat of Oromo Democracy

The Gada System is not just a governance structure; it is the living soul of the Oromo people. For centuries, the Gada has guided the Oromo in matters of leadership, justice, social organization, and spirituality. Every eight years, power transitions peacefully from one generation-set (Luba) to another, ensuring that no single group holds authority indefinitely. This cyclical system fosters accountability, renewal, and unity.

Under Gada, every Oromo male passes through age-sets, learning responsibility, wisdom, and leadership. Women, too, play vital roles in rituals and social life, their voices echoing in the Gada assemblies. The Abba Gada, elected by consensus, leads with the counsel of elders and the will of the people. Laws are debated in open air under the shade of the Odaa tree, symbolizing transparency and inclusiveness.
